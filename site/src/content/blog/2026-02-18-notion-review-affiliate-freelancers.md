## Where Notion Falls Short

**Learning curve:** Notion has a steep initial learning curve. The block-based interface, database concepts, and linked views confuse new users. Expect to spend 2‚Äì5 hours getting comfortable before you're productive.

**Mobile app:** The mobile experience is noticeably worse than desktop. It's functional but slow to load and awkward for quick captures. Keep a simple notes app for on-the-go captures, then transfer to Notion.

**Offline mode:** Notion requires an internet connection. If you work in spots with poor connectivity, this is a dealbreaker.

**Not the best task manager:** For complex project management with dependencies, time tracking, and Gantt charts, dedicated tools like ClickUp or Asana are better. Notion's task management is good for solopreneurs but gets clunky with teams.

**Search:** Notion's search is improving but still lags behind Google Drive or Evernote for finding specific content quickly across large workspaces.
